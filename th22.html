Employment Types Comparison

Criteria Full-Time Part-Time Contract Temporary
Duration Indefinite (ongoing) Indefinite (ongoing) Fixed term (project-based) Short-term (weeks to months)
Legal Status Employee (benefits eligible) Employee (limited benefits) Independent contractor Employee or contractor
Compensation Salary or hourly with benefits Hourly with limited benefits Project rate or hourly (no benefits) Typically hourly (minimal benefits)
Purpose Core business operations Reduced staffing needs Specialized skills or projects Seasonal or interim coverage
Commitment Level High (40+ hours weekly) Medium (15-30 hours weekly) Varies by contract terms Limited to specific timeframe

Full-Time Employment

Duration: Indefinite (ongoing)

Legal Status: Employee (benefits eligible)

Compensation: Salary or hourly with benefits

Purpose: Core business operations

Commitment Level: High (40+ hours weekly)

Part-Time Employment

Duration: Indefinite (ongoing)

Legal Status: Employee (limited benefits)

Compensation: Hourly with limited benefits

Purpose: Reduced staffing needs

Commitment Level: Medium (15-30 hours weekly)

Contract Employment

Duration: Fixed term (project-based)

Legal Status: Independent contractor

Compensation: Project rate or hourly (no benefits)

Purpose: Specialized skills or projects

Commitment Level: Varies by contract terms

Temporary Employment

Duration: Short-term (weeks to months)

Legal Status: Employee or contractor

Compensation: Typically hourly (minimal benefits)

Purpose: Seasonal or interim coverage

Commitment Level: Limited to specific timeframe
