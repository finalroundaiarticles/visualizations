Palindrome Linked List - Optimized Approach

Step 1: Finding the Middle with Slow/Fast Pointers
Slow
1
→
2
→
Fast
3
→
2
→
1
How it works:
• Slow pointer moves 1 step at a time
• Fast pointer moves 2 steps at a time
• When fast pointer reaches the end, slow pointer is at the middle
• This helps us find where to split the list for comparison
Step 2: Reversing the Second Half
1
→
2
→
3
→
2
→
1
Reversing Process:
• We reverse the second half of the linked list (from middle to end)
• Original: 1→2→3→2→1
• After reversing second half: 1→2→3←2←1
• Now we can compare from both ends moving inward
Step 3: Comparing First Half with Reversed Second Half
First Half
1
→
2
VS
Reversed Second Half
1
→
2
Comparison Process:
• Compare nodes from first half with nodes from reversed second half
• 1 == 1 ✓ (Match!)
• 2 == 2 ✓ (Match!)
• All nodes match, so it's a palindrome!
• Time Complexity: O(n), Space Complexity: O(1)
