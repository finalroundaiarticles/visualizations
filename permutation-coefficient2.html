Factorial vs. Optimized Calculation Comparison

Comparing two methods to calculate P(5,2) - Permutation of 5 items taken 2 at a time

Factorial Method

The formula for permutation P(n,r) is:

P(n,r) = n! / (n-r)!

Substituting n=5, r=2:

P(5,2) = 5! / (5-2)!

P(5,2) = 5! / 3!

Calculate 5!:

5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1

5! = 120

Calculate 3!:

3! = 3 × 2 × 1

3! = 6

Divide 5! by 3!:

P(5,2) = 120 / 6

↓
P(5,2) = 20
Optimized Method

For P(n,r), we can use an optimized formula:

P(n,r) = n × (n-1) × (n-2) × ... × (n-r+1)

Substituting n=5, r=2:

P(5,2) = 5 × (5-1) × (5-2) × ... × (5-2+1)

P(5,2) = 5 × 4

We only need to multiply the first r terms:

P(5,2) = 5 × 4

Calculate directly:

P(5,2) = 5 × 4 = 20

The optimized calculation is simpler and faster:

↓
P(5,2) = 20
